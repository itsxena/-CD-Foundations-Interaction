However odd it may seem, he never suspected the truth; it burst upon him suddenly.

He realized that he was unable to remember the shapes, sounds, and colors of his dreams; there were no shapes, colors, or sounds, nor were the dreams dreams. They were his reality, a reality beyond silence and sight, and therefore beyond memory. [This realization threw him into even greater consternation than the fact that from the hour of his death he had been struggling in a whirlwind of senseless images.]
